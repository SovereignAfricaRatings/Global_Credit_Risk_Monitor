Analyst Briefing

Base Case: Status Quo with Simmering Tensions

Probability: 65%

Our baseline assumption factors in continued diplomatic friction and proxy engagements without direct, sustained military confrontation between the US and Iran. Oil markets maintain a geopolitical risk premium, but physical supply remains largely uninterrupted. Credit conditions globally follow their current trajectory of a soft landing, with African sovereigns gradually returning to international capital markets.

African Sovereign Vulnerability Matrix

A Middle East conflict triggers two simultaneous shocks for emerging markets: a terms-of-trade shock (higher import bills for oil importers) and a financial shock (capital flight to safe-haven assets, spiking borrowing costs).

⚠ High Vulnerability (Oil Importers with High Debt)

Countries like Kenya, Egypt, and Senegal face severe rating pressure. Higher fuel import bills drain FX reserves, while surging US Treasury yields effectively close market access for refinancing Eurobonds.

✔ Mixed/Positive Outlook (Oil Exporters)

Nigeria and Angola experience near-term fiscal windfalls from elevated oil prices. However, in severe scenarios, global demand destruction limits volume, and structural economic issues remain unaddressed.
